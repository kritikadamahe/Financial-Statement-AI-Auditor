\begin{abstract}
Financial statement fraud poses a significant threat to global markets. Traditional auditing methods frequently struggle to detect complex anomalies hidden within massive volumes of financial documentation, while purely quantitative machine learning models suffer from unacceptably high false positive rates. This paper presents FinAuditAI, a novel multi-modal explainable artificial intelligence (XAI) framework. Our approach fuses quantitative temporal anomaly detection with qualitative natural language processing (NLP) to reduce false positives and provide interpretable audit trails. The numerical pipeline leverages a 6-layer ensemble, including a PyTorch-based Long Short-Term Memory Autoencoder (LSTM-AE). To address high false positive rates, we introduce a Cross-Modality NLP Veto module powered by a Large Language Model (LLM) that evaluates the risk profile of Management's Discussion and Analysis (MD\&A) texts. Evaluated on a synthetic dataset of 1,200 companies and validated against 19 real-world SEC Accounting and Auditing Enforcement Releases (AAER), the NLP Veto significantly enhances detection precision from 55.0\% to 98.0\% while maintaining an overall accuracy of 98.0\% and an F1-score of 0.93.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
Financial statement fraud causes billions of dollars in losses annually and undermines the integrity of global markets \cite{12}. Detecting manipulation is inherently difficult because fraudsters employ sophisticated techniques designed to appear mathematically sound in isolation \cite{4}.

While classical machine learning (ML) techniques have been applied to this domain, they suffer from two critical limitations: lack of temporal context and prohibitively high false positive rates. Traditional models often treat financial periods independently, failing to capture subtle multi-year income smoothing \cite{16}. Furthermore, purely quantitative models frequently flag legitimate, high-growth companies as fraudulent, causing alert fatigue \cite{11}. 

FinAuditAI advances the state-of-the-art by implementing a comprehensive 6-layer numerical anomaly detection ensemble that includes a PyTorch LSTM-AE capable of modeling 5-year chronological dependencies. Crucially, we pair this numerical engine with a Cross-Modality NLP Veto module. By semantically analyzing unstructured textual data in Annual Reports using an LLM, the system contextualizes numerical anomalies and "vetoes" false alarms triggered by healthy corporate events, dramatically improving precision.

\section{Related Work}
Early computational research relied on static financial ratios evaluated through supervised classifiers like SVMs \cite{11}, \cite{16}. However, these require scarce labeled data. Consequently, unsupervised anomaly detection (e.g., Isolation Forests \cite{7}) gained traction. Recently, deep learning autoencoders have been employed to detect accounting anomalies \cite{3}, \cite{15}. However, standard dense autoencoders often ignore sequential reporting dynamics. 

Simultaneously, researchers explored textual analysis of corporate disclosures. Loughran and McDonald \cite{1} demonstrated that linguistic tone is highly indicative of corporate liability. Bao et al. \cite{5} and Craja et al. \cite{2} proposed multi-modal approaches combining text and numbers. FinAuditAI builds upon this paradigm but introduces a novel "veto" architecture where NLP acts as an independent risk-moderation layer specifically designed to suppress false positives.

\section{Methodology}

\subsection{System Architecture}
The FinAuditAI pipeline is structured as a dual-path architecture converging into a final decision matrix. It ingests both structured financial data (CSV) and unstructured reports (PDF).

\begin{figure}[htbp]
\centerline{\includegraphics[width=\columnwidth]{architecture diagram.png}}
\caption{System Architecture of FinAuditAI. The upper branch ingests numerical data through a 6-layer ensemble. The lower branch extracts unstructured text for LLM semantic analysis. The Cross-Modality NLP Veto fuses both to produce the final classification.}
\label{fig:architecture}
\end{figure}

\subsection{Feature Engineering}
The system extracts 11 core financial metrics over a 5-year period. We engineer 66+ features capturing distribution and temporal dynamics, including YoY volatility, standard liquidity ratios ($CR_t$, $DE_t$), and slope divergence. Slope divergence ($\frac{\beta_{Rev} - \beta_{OCF}}{|\mu_{Rev}|}$) identifies companies booking revenues without generating commensurate cash.

\subsection{Quantitative Anomaly Detection Layers}
The numerical pipeline utilizes a weighted ensemble:

\textbf{1. Benford's Law (15\%):} Evaluates leading digit distributions.

\textbf{2. Isolation Forest (25\%):} Isolates anomalies in high-dimensional space based on the expected path length $E(h(x))$ \cite{7}.

\textbf{3. Local Outlier Factor (20\%):} Identifies density-based local outliers \cite{9}.

\textbf{4. One-Class SVM (15\%):} Learns a non-linear decision boundary bounding normal corporate profiles \cite{8}.

\textbf{5. LSTM Autoencoder (15\%):} A Sequence-to-Sequence model capturing chronological dependencies over $T=5$ years. The encoder processes the sequence using LSTM gates: $f_t = \sigma(W_f x_t + U_f h_{t-1} + b_f)$, etc. The network is trained on normal companies to minimize MSE reconstruction loss:
\begin{equation}
    L_{MSE} = \frac{1}{T \cdot F} \sum_{t=1}^{T} \sum_{f=1}^{F} (x_{t,f} - \hat{x}_{t,f})^2
\end{equation}

\textbf{6. Temporal Trajectory (10\%):} A rule-based layer tracking hard violations of accounting logic.

\subsection{Cross-Modality NLP Veto}
Raw MD\&A text is extracted and analyzed by an LLM (Gemini 1.5 Flash). The LLM assigns a standardized Risk Score ($R_{NLP} \in [0, 100]$) based on evasive language and undisclosed liquidity concerns.

\begin{algorithm}
\caption{Dual-Path Inference with NLP Veto}
\begin{algorithmic}[1]
\REQUIRE Financial Data $X_{num}$, PDF Report $X_{text}$
\STATE $S_{Ens} \leftarrow \text{ComputeEnsemble}(X_{num})$
\STATE $R_{NLP} \leftarrow \text{LLMRiskScore}(X_{text})$
\STATE $\tau \leftarrow \text{85th percentile threshold}$
\IF{$S_{Ens} > \tau$ \AND $R_{NLP} \ge 30$}
    \STATE $Prediction \leftarrow \text{Anomaly}$
\ELSE
    \STATE $Prediction \leftarrow \text{Normal}$
\ENDIF
\RETURN $Prediction$
\end{algorithmic}
\end{algorithm}

\subsection{Explainability via SHAP}
To satisfy regulatory requirements, the system integrates SHapley Additive exPlanations (SHAP), providing a direct mapping of which specific financial ratios triggered the fraud alert in the Isolation Forest model.

\section{Experimental Setup}

\subsection{Dataset Generation and Validation}
Due to the scarcity of comprehensive financial fraud datasets, we generated a synthetic dataset via Monte Carlo simulation (1,000 normal, 200 fraudulent companies, 5 years each). Fraud typologies included revenue inflation, Benford violations, earnings-cash divergence, debt hiding, and revenue smoothing. 

For empirical validation, we tested on 19 real-world cases from the SEC EDGAR database (9 frauds including Enron, WorldCom, and Wirecard, and 10 stable peers). A custom scale-normalization algorithm compressed absolute scales while preserving underlying ratios.

\section{Results and Evaluation}

\subsection{Performance on Synthetic Data}
The baseline model (without NLP Veto) successfully detected subtle fraud patterns but at the cost of high false positives. The LSTM Autoencoder successfully converged, decaying exponentially to an MSE of 0.008 by epoch 60.

\subsection{Impact of the NLP Veto}
The integration of the NLP Veto proved to be the most significant architectural improvement. By evaluating the textual risk context, the system eliminated nearly all false positives generated by the highly sensitive numerical ensemble.

\begin{figure}[htbp]

\section{Conclusion}
FinAuditAI successfully addresses the pervasive issue of high false positive rates in auditing anomaly detection. By combining a 6-layer numerical ensemble with a PyTorch LSTM Autoencoder for chronological modeling, it effectively captures multi-year fraud dynamics. Crucially, the integration of an LLM-powered Cross-Modality NLP Veto drastically reduced false positives, catapulting precision to 98.0\%. The system demonstrated near-perfect empirical performance on major real-world SEC fraud cases, and the inclusion of SHAP explainability provides forensic accountants with transparent audit trails.
